Study In A Minor  
  
  *This is a simple but effective song. It is simple one of the most*  
  *famous etudes (spanish for "study") by Dionisio Aguado. It is in the key*  
  *of A minor and is in 2/4 time. It could easily be in 4/4 but the accents*  
  *would be in the wrong place. Each note represents a sixteenth note (with*  
  *the exception of the two quarter notes in the song denoted by a "q" above*  
  *the note) and the accent should be on 1 and 2. You can see the*  
  *greater-than symbols ('&gt;') below the 1st and 2nd beat of the first few*  
  *measures. Then it says "sim." which stands for simile which means "keep*  
  *on doing that all throughout the song unless otherwise directed".*
